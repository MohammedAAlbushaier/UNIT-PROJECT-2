Our History

God honored the Arabs by choosing from among them His Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) to be the Seal of the Prophets. He then honored the Arabian Peninsula by revealing His clear Book and guidance to the worlds on its soil.

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia was honored to bear the responsibility of caring for the Book of God, preserving it, serving it, printing it, and publishing it. It established the King Fahd Complex for the Printing of the Holy Qur'an, which is considered the greatest institution for preserving, serving, and publishing the Qur'an in the history of Islam.

One of the fruits of this assignment and honor was that the people of goodness and the Qur'an in Ahsa, the center of knowledge and scholars, called upon one of the doors of honor to serve the Qur'an and care for the worn-out Qur'ans, as they are passed around by thousands of hands over the years, to gain the reward of recitation and memorization, which requires their maintenance, renewal, and redistribution.

Thus, the establishment of the "His Excellency Dr. Muhammad bin Hassan Al-Jaber Center for the Care of the Qur'an in Al-Ahsa" was established by His Eminence Sheikh Khalid bin Muhammad Al-Maghribi - may God have mercy on him - in the month of Ramadan in the year 1427 AH, under the umbrella of the Qur'an Memorization Association in Al-Ahsa (Khair), and with the support and thanks for its establishment from the Governorate of Al-Ahsa.

In 1446/2/1 AH - AD, the Care Association was established in Al-Ahsa, authorized under the supervisory authority of the Ministry of Islamic Affairs.

Our Distribution Process

1

Collecting The Qur'an

Where the copies of the Qur'an that need care are collected from the mosques of the governorate's cities and villages, from Qur'an memorization circles, and from schools.

2

Care and Maintenance

  1. Receiving the collected copies of the Qur'an and storing them in the warehouse
  2. Sorting and classifying them according to their needs
  3. Inspecting the copies, removing any writing, and ensuring their cleanliness
  4. Replacing damaged pages with intact ones
  5. Re-weaving and packaging
  6. Arranging the preserved copies of the Qur'an in boxes, in preparation for distribution
3

Distribution

Our organization redistributes the renewed copies of the Qur'an to the entities that are most in need of them and benefit from them - inside and outside the Kingdom - including mosques, prayer halls, and memorization circles.
